Welcome to Centra Java

Central Java, a province in the island of Java, is a region steeped in rich culture and history. Known for its stunning landscapes, ancient temples, and vibrant arts scene, Central Java is a must-visit destination for any traveler. The region is home to the magnificent Borobudur Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most famous Buddhist temples in the world. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by attending traditional ceremonies, trying local cuisines, and exploring the region's many temples and museums. With its warm hospitality and breathtaking scenery, Central Java is a place where history and culture come alive.

The Magnificent Ancient Dance

Bali, an island of Indonesia, is home to a rich cultural heritage, which includes traditional dances. One of the most well-known dances is the Legong dance, which is a classical dance that originated in the 19th century. The dance is characterized by intricate footwork, expressive hand gestures, and hypnotic rhythms. The Legong dance is often performed by young girls, and is a popular tourist attraction in Bali. The dance is a testament to the island's rich cultural history, and is a must-see for anyone visiting Bali.

The Majestic Ancient Ritual

The Surakarta Palace, also known as the Kraton of Surakarta, is a traditional Javanese palace located in Surakarta, Central Java, Indonesia. The palace is a symbol of the rich cultural heritage of the Javanese people and is a popular tourist destination. The palace is home to many important cultural artifacts, including traditional batik and kris (traditional Javanese dagger) making. The palace is also a place of great historical significance, as it was the site of the Java War (1825-1830) and the place where the Javanese people resisted the Dutch colonial occupation. The palace is a living example of Javanese culture and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in learning about the history and culture of the Javanese people.
